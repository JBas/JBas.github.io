CMU Mars Ice

Club Project

My involvement in the design of the CMU drilling robot for the NASA Mars Ice Challenge includes creating design blocks in EagleCAD as well as selecting and testing components; this culminated in my design and assembly of the robot's encoder interface pcb. I also spearheaded the design and development of the software that controlled and monitored the robot (everything in src/ except client-side code). This included interfacing with the robot's thermometer, ammeter, water level sensor, force sensor, and encoder data. Some challenges encountered during the software development included only having about 3 weeks to implement, unit test, and integration test; however, we were able to produce a minimum viable product that successfully interpreted and executed a subset of our desired commands.

Image Hill Climber

Personal Project

In an effort to learn more about genetic algorithms and hill climber algorithms, I designed a population that attempts to reconstruct a given image from polygons with at least N (user-defined) vertices using a range of RGBA values. Implemented originally in plain Java and later refactored in Processing, the program compares the current and target image on a per-pixel basis. Although I enjoy coming back to this project every so often, after seeing my initial results, I learned that genetic and hill climbing algorithms are not the most efficient nor do they always find global maxima.
